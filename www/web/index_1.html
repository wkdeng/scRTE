About scARE (single cell Atlas of Retrotransposable Elements)
Recent studies have revealed the important role of retrotransposons in neurodegenerative disease (Copley and Shorter 2023). Abnormal activation or repression of RTEs have been observed in multiple neurodegenerative diseases, including Alzheimer’s disease (Sun et al. 2018), Parkinson’s disease (Baeken, Moosmann, and Hajieva 2020), multiple sclerosis (Heinrich et al. 2019) and so on. However, RTE dynamics in neurodegenerative diseases are under explored. With advances of single cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) technology, the community has accumulated large amount of single cell transcriptome profiles in neurodegenerative disease (Ahmadi et al. 2021). In this work, we compiled scRNA-seq dataset from 11 studies in Alzheimer’s disease, Parkinson’s disease, and multiple sclerosis, to reveal RTE dynamic in disease and cell types. In total, we quantified RTE expression in more than 600,000 cells and built single cell atlas of retrotransposon (scARE) to host these data. In scARE, we provide various search and browse options, including search RTE, gene, disease or dataset by single term and multiple terms, browse by RTE, cell type, disease, and dataset. Furthermore, we implemented discover option to highlight most expressed, most differential expressed or co-expressed RTEs in given conditions. Overall, scARE provides handful tool for exploration of RTE dynamic in neurodegenerative diseases.
